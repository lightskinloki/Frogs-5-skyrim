Foreword
"There was a time I loved being a blood-sucking creature of the night. It is not impossible to live only at night—merely occasionally inconvenient. And I wouldn't have to kill humans every night, merely warm-blooded creatures. Orcs have a delicious, rich Bharathi blood. Rats are a little sweet for the only meal of the night. Werewolves are a real treat, almost decadent—the tincture between human and beast, a real gourmet's delight."
These words, recovered from the journal of an anonymous vampire, illustrate the complex relationship these creatures have with their condition. Some embrace it fully, finding pleasure in the hunt and the taste of various bloods. Others struggle against their nature, seeking redemption or cure. All, however, are bound by the same fundamental truth: they are no longer truly alive, yet they persist, feeding on the life force of others to maintain their undead existence.
When night settles over Tamriel, horrors emerge from the darkness. In the stillness of night, unnaturally strong and fast, a vampire can sink their teeth into a victim before they can fight back. The fortunate die quickly from the bite; the unfortunate contract the vile affliction and begin their own transformation into creatures of the night.
This codex represents the most comprehensive study of vampirism ever compiled, drawing from centuries of research, firsthand accounts, survivor testimonies, and even confessions from vampires themselves. It is my hope that this knowledge will serve scholars, adventurers, and common folk alike in understanding and, when necessary, combating these creatures who walk the line between the living and the dead.

Chapter I: The Fundamental Nature of Vampirism
Understanding the Vampire
The vampire occupies a unique and terrifying position within the hierarchy of undead creatures. Unlike the mindless draugr that guard ancient tombs, or the shambling skeletons raised by necromancers, vampires retain their intelligence, memories, and personalities from their mortal lives. This makes them infinitely more dangerous than their lesser undead cousins, as they can plan, scheme, and adapt to changing circumstances.
Fear and distrust are all that can be expected for anyone who has contracted the affliction of vampirism. This universal revulsion drives many vampires to band together in clans, creating strongholds around powerful bloodlines where they can find acceptance among their own kind. The isolation from mortal society often breeds contempt and hatred in return, creating a cycle of violence and mistrust that has persisted for millennia.
Physical Manifestations
Instead of undergoing beastly transformations or suffering from draugr-like deterioration, most vampires maintain their mortal appearance when properly fed. This camouflage allows them to move undetected among the living, making them far more dangerous than creatures whose monstrous nature is immediately apparent. Their more horrific forms are revealed only when they have been starved of blood for extended periods.
Well-fed vampires develop certain telltale characteristics that the trained eye can identify:
* A distinctly pale complexion, as if they have not seen sunlight in years
* Fangs that remain hidden behind their lips until feeding time
* Eyes that may appear more intense or predatory than normal
* An unnatural stillness when at rest, lacking the subtle movements of breathing that characterize the living
* Skin that feels cold to the touch, though this is often disguised through careful attention to clothing and environment
The Hunger
The defining characteristic of all vampires is their insatiable hunger for blood. This is not merely a physical need but a spiritual compulsion that grows stronger the longer it remains unsatisfied. A well-fed vampire may appear almost human, but one who has gone without feeding becomes increasingly monstrous in both appearance and behavior.
The hunger affects different vampires in various ways:
* Some describe it as a burning thirst that consumes all other thoughts
* Others experience it as a gnawing emptiness that can only be filled by the life force of others
* Many report that the hunger becomes easier to manage over time, though it never truly disappears
* Ancient vampires often develop sophisticated methods of feeding that minimize their exposure and maximize their satisfaction
Supernatural Abilities
All vampires, regardless of bloodline, possess certain enhanced capabilities that mark them as creatures beyond mortal limitations:
Enhanced Physical Prowess: Vampires possess strength and speed that far exceed mortal capabilities. They can move with blur-like quickness, allowing them to strike before victims can react. Their strength allows them to tear through armor and break bones with ease.
Accelerated Healing: Wounds that would prove fatal to mortals heal with supernatural speed in vampires. Minor cuts close within minutes, while more serious injuries may heal completely within hours. This regeneration is fueled by blood consumption—well-fed vampires heal faster than those who are starved.
Extended Lifespan: Perhaps the most significant aspect of vampirism is the virtual immortality it grants. Vampires can live for thousands of years if they are careful, accumulating knowledge, power, and influence over centuries. Some of the oldest vampires remember events from the Merethic Era, making them living repositories of ancient history.
Night Vision: All vampires can see clearly in complete darkness, though this ability varies in effectiveness among different bloodlines. Some possess vision sharp enough to read fine text by starlight alone.
Resistance to Disease and Poison: The undead nature of vampires grants them immunity to most mortal ailments. Plagues that devastate mortal populations pass over vampire communities without effect.
Weaknesses and Vulnerabilities
Despite their supernatural advantages, vampires are not invincible. They possess several notable weaknesses that vary in severity among different bloodlines:
Sunlight Sensitivity: While the degree varies significantly among different vampire strains, most experience some form of discomfort or weakness when exposed to direct sunlight. Some bloodlines suffer immediate and severe damage, while others merely lose some of their supernatural abilities during daylight hours.
Vulnerability to Blessed Items: Holy symbols, blessed weapons, and consecrated ground can cause severe discomfort or actual harm to vampires, though the effectiveness depends on the strength of the blessing and the power of the vampire in question.
Wooden Stakes: A wooden stake driven through the heart can paralyze or destroy a vampire, though this method requires precise placement and considerable force.
Fire Damage: Vampires are particularly susceptible to fire damage, which can cause wounds that heal more slowly than normal injuries.
Running Water: Some bloodlines cannot cross running water without experiencing weakness or discomfort, though this weakness is not universal among all vampire strains.
Invitation Requirement: Certain types of vampires cannot enter private dwellings without being invited by the occupants, though this restriction typically applies only to homes, not public buildings or abandoned structures.

Chapter II: The Dark Genesis of Vampirism
The Cosmic Conflict
To understand vampirism, one must first comprehend the divine conflict that birthed this curse. The creation of vampirism stems from an ancient rivalry between two powerful entities: Arkay, the Aedra of life and death, and Molag Bal, the Daedric Prince of domination and corruption.
Arkay took great pride in his sphere of influence—the natural cycle of life and death that governed all living beings in Nirn. No man, mer, or beast could escape the eventual call to death, and in this universal truth, Arkay found satisfaction and purpose. The cycle was perfect, inevitable, and just.
Molag Bal, whose sphere encompasses the wanton oppression and entrapment of mortal souls, viewed Arkay's pride as an opportunity for spite and corruption. The Daedric Prince sought to create beings who could mock Arkay's beloved cycle by escaping death itself, becoming trapped in a state of undeath that neither truly lived nor properly died.
This cosmic conflict would have devastating consequences for the mortal races of Tamriel, as Molag Bal set about creating a perversion of the natural order that would plague the world for millennia to come.
The First Victim: Lamae Bal
The origin of vampirism begins with one of the most tragic and horrific acts of violence in Tamriel's history. During the early days when the continent was still young and the Nedic tribes roamed the land, Molag Bal descended to Nirn with malicious intent.
The Daedric Prince encountered Lamae Bal, a woman of the Nedic tribes, living peacefully among her people. What followed was an act of unspeakable brutality that would echo through the ages. Molag Bal ravaged Lamae in body and spirit before departing, leaving her broken and dying on the ground.
Nomadic travelers discovered Lamae and, moved by compassion, attempted to nurse her back to health. Despite their best efforts, she appeared to succumb to her injuries and died from the traumatic ordeal. The nomads, following their cultural traditions, prepared to cremade her body according to their customs.
The First Transformation
As the funeral pyre was lit and flames began to lick at Lamae's body, something unprecedented occurred. The wounds that had seemingly killed her began healing with incredible, unnatural speed. The nomads watched in horror as their presumed-dead charge stirred and then rose from what should have been her death bed.
But Lamae was no longer the woman they had tried to save. The trauma of her ordeal, combined with Molag Bal's corrupt influence, had transformed her into something entirely new and terrible. Overwhelmed by an rage and hunger she had never experienced, Lamae fell upon her rescuers in a vicious fury.
The massacre that followed was unlike anything the world had seen. Lamae, now possessed of supernatural strength and an insatiable bloodlust, slaughtered the entire nomadic tribe in a dark display of her newfound power. When the carnage ended and she regained her senses, she realized what she had become: the first vampire, the first Daughter of Coldharbour.
The Plea to Arkay
Initially, Lamae was horrified by her actions and her undead state. The woman who had been violated and transformed against her will sought redemption and reversal of her condition. In desperation, she pleaded with Arkay to undo Molag Bal's work, begging the Aedra to restore the natural order and grant her the death that had been denied to her.
Arkay's response was silence. Whether unable or unwilling to reverse what Molag Bal had wrought, the Aedra of death left Lamae forsaken, abandoned to her new existence without hope of redemption or return to mortality.
The Birth of a Bloodline
Arkay's rejection had profound consequences. Feeling abandoned by the very force she had hoped would save her, Lamae's initial horror transformed into acceptance and then into vengeful embrace of her new nature. If she was to be forever denied Arkay's peace, then she would ensure that others shared her fate.
Lamae doubled down on her vampirism, actively spreading the disease throughout Tamriel. In doing so, she fulfilled Molag Bal's original intention: creating a mockery of Arkay's beloved cycle of life and death. Each person she turned became another affront to the natural order, another soul trapped in the liminal space between life and death.
Alternative Creation Myths
While Lamae Bal's story represents the most widely accepted origin of vampirism, other cultures and regions have developed their own explanations for the curse's beginnings. Dunmer culture, in particular, presents a different account of how vampirism came to exist.
According to Dunmer beliefs, the first vampire was not created from a mortal victim but was instead spawned by Molag Bal from a defeated Daedra Lord, temple saint, or other powerful supernatural creature. This origin story suggests a more deliberate creation process, with vampirism designed as a weapon or tool rather than arising from a crime of passion and spite.
The existence of multiple origin stories raises intriguing questions about the true nature of vampirism's beginnings. It's entirely possible that both accounts contain elements of truth. Molag Bal, being a Daedric Prince of considerable power and cunning, may have created vampirism through multiple methods, ensuring that the curse would spread through various vectors and prove impossible to completely eradicate.
Many prominent vampire families and bloodlines claim direct descent from pacts made with Molag Bal himself, suggesting that the Daedric Prince has continued to create vampires throughout history, not merely relying on the spread of the original curse through Lamae's bloodline.
The Continuing Legacy
Regardless of which origin story holds the most truth, the consequences of vampirism's creation continue to ripple through Tamriel's history. The curse has spread to every province, adapted to different cultures and environments, and evolved into numerous distinct bloodlines, each with its own characteristics and abilities.
Lamae Bal herself has become a figure of worship among some vampire cults, revered as the Blood Matron who brought the gift of immortality to mortals. Others view her as a tragic figure, the first victim of Molag Bal's cruelty who became the unwilling mother of all vampires. Still others see her as a cautionary tale about the consequences of divine abandonment and the corruption that can arise when mortals are left to face cosmic forces alone.

Chapter III: The Mechanics of Vampiric Transmission
Primary Infection Vectors
Vampirism spreads through Tamriel via several documented methods, though direct blood contact through wounds remains the most common form of transmission. Understanding these vectors is crucial for both those seeking to avoid infection and those attempting to track the spread of vampiric influence in their regions.
Bite Transmission: The most frequently documented method occurs when a vampire's fangs pierce the victim's skin, introducing vampiric essence directly into the bloodstream. However, not every bite results in infection—factors such as the vampire's age, power level, and intent all influence the likelihood of transmission.
Claw and Weapon Wounds: Vampires can transmit their curse through any wound they inflict, not merely bites. Scratches from claws, cuts from vampiric weapons, or even contact with vampiric blood in open wounds can potentially spread the affliction.
Ritual Transmission: Some bloodlines practice deliberate infection through ceremonial means, such as the Rite of the Scion associated with Lamae Bal's descendants. These rituals often involve the complete replacement of the subject's blood with vampiric blood, ensuring successful transformation.
Rare Reproductive Transmission: Historical accounts describe extremely rare cases of vampires impregnating living women, though sources remain inconclusive about whether the resulting children are born mortal, vampiric, or something entirely different. Such cases are so uncommon that many scholars question their veracity.
Survival Rates
The brutal reality of vampiric encounters is that many victims do not survive their initial contact with these predators. Vampires, when hunting, often drain their prey completely, leaving nothing but desiccated corpses. The survival rate for vampiric encounters depends on several factors:
Vampire's Intent: A vampire seeking only sustenance will typically drain the victim completely. Those seeking to create progeny or servants may exercise restraint, allowing the victim to survive and transform.
Victim's Constitution: Individuals with strong constitutions, magical protections, or divine favor may survive encounters that would prove fatal to others.
Interruption of Feeding: Many vampire attack survivors owe their lives to interruptions—other people arriving, dawn breaking, or the vampire being driven off by circumstances.
Vampire's Age and Power: Older, more powerful vampires have greater control over their feeding and can more easily regulate the amount of blood they drain and vampiric essence they transmit.
The Disease Strains
Vampirism manifests through numerous distinct strains, each associated with different bloodlines and exhibiting unique characteristics. The most commonly documented strains include:
Porphyric Hemophilia: One of the most widespread strains, characterized by increased sensitivity to sunlight and enhanced strength during nighttime hours. Victims experience the classic 72-hour incubation period before full transformation.
Sanguinare Vampiris: Another common strain found primarily in northern regions. This variant often presents with enhanced cold resistance but increased vulnerability to fire and holy magic.
Noxiphilic Sanguivoria: Associated with Lamae Bal's direct descendants, this rare strain grants immunity to sunlight but comes with an insatiable hunger that drives victims to feed more frequently than other vampire types.
Each strain carries different symptoms, abilities, and weaknesses, making universal treatments or protections difficult to develop. Regional variations also exist within these broader categories, likely influenced by local magical conditions, prevalent diseases, or environmental factors.
The Transformation Timeline
The process of vampiric transformation follows a predictable pattern, though individual experiences may vary based on the strain contracted and the victim's constitution:
Hours 0-72: The Silent Period During the first three days following infection, victims display no overt physical symptoms of their condition. However, they begin experiencing:
* Recurring nightmares, often involving blood, darkness, or the vampire who infected them
* Chronic insomnia, finding it difficult to achieve restful sleep
* Gradual loss of appetite for normal food and drink
* Increasing sensitivity to bright lights
* Subtle changes in body temperature, becoming noticeably cooler to the touch
This period represents the last chance for easy cure of the affliction through magical or alchemical means.
Hour 72: The Death At exactly the 72-hour mark, the infected individual undergoes a process that can only be described as death. Their heart stops, breathing ceases, and by all medical measures, they are deceased. This death is not permanent but represents the transition from life to undeath.
The Awakening The duration of death varies dramatically among individuals. Some vampires report awakening within hours of their death, while others describe waking days later, sometimes to find themselves already buried or entombed. The awakening is typically accompanied by:
* Overwhelming hunger for blood
* Confusion and disorientation
* Enhanced senses that can be initially overwhelming
* Realization of their transformed state
* Instinctive understanding of their new abilities
First Feeding The newly risen vampire experiences an almost irresistible compulsion to feed. This first feeding often determines much about their future existence—those who resist or find alternative sources may develop more control over their hunger, while those who kill during their first feeding often struggle with bloodlust throughout their undead existence.
Factors Affecting Transformation
Several factors influence both the likelihood of infection and the characteristics of the resulting vampire:
Age of the Infecting Vampire: Older vampires carry more potent strains and are more likely to successfully transmit their curse. However, they also have greater control over whether transmission occurs.
Victim's Magical Resistance: Individuals with natural or trained magical resistance may fight off the infection during the 72-hour window or experience delayed or modified transformations.
Divine Protection: Those under the active protection of Aedra or Daedra may find their transformation altered or prevented entirely, though such protection is rare and often comes with its own costs.
Regional Magical Influences: Areas with strong magical emanations, such as near Dwemer ruins or sites of magical catastrophe, may influence the transformation process in unpredictable ways.
Emotional State: The victim's mental and emotional condition at the time of infection may influence their vampiric personality. Those infected in states of rage, fear, or despair often become more aggressive vampires, while those infected while calm or prepared may retain more of their original personality.

Chapter IV: The Complex Art of Vampiric Cures
Dispelling Common Misconceptions
Perhaps the most dangerous misconception about vampirism is the widespread belief that it represents an incurable condition. This fallacy has led to the unnecessary deaths of countless individuals who might have been saved, and has driven many newly infected vampires to embrace their condition rather than seek treatment.
The truth is significantly more complex: vampirism can indeed be cured, but the methods are difficult, dangerous, and often unreliable. The variety of vampire strains and bloodlines means that no single cure works universally, and what saves one vampire may prove ineffective or even fatal for another.
Divine Intervention Methods
Direct Appeals to Molag Bal The most dangerous but potentially effective cure involves approaching the source of vampirism itself. In documented cases, vampires have contacted Molag Bal directly, offering to complete tasks or fulfill bargains in exchange for release from their curse. This method carries enormous risks:
* The tasks demanded are invariably morally reprehensible and dangerous
* Molag Bal may demand the petitioner commit acts that corrupt them beyond redemption
* Failure to complete the assigned task often results in punishments worse than vampirism itself
* Success does not guarantee the promised cure will be granted
Vaermina's Role Intriguingly, research has revealed that Vaermina, the Daedric Prince of dreams and nightmares, provides the actual cures that Molag Bal distributes to successful petitioners. This connection helps explain why both newly infected individuals and established vampires experience particular nightmares—Vaermina's sphere of influence extends into the vampiric condition itself.
This revelation suggests that approaching Vaermina directly might yield better results than dealing with Molag Bal, though documented cases of such attempts are extremely rare and their outcomes poorly recorded.
Bloodline Destruction Cures
The Blood Father Method One of the most dramatic but effective cures involves killing the "blood father" or founding vampire of an entire bloodline. When successful, this method can cure every member of that bloodline simultaneously, regardless of how many generations removed they are from the original vampire.
This method carries significant risks and complications:
* Identifying the true blood father can be extremely difficult, as vampire genealogies are often deliberately obscured
* Blood fathers are typically ancient, powerful vampires with centuries of experience and supernatural abilities
* The cure affects all bloodline members simultaneously, which can result in mass deaths as newly mortal individuals die of old age
* Some bloodlines may have multiple blood fathers or complex inheritance patterns that make this method ineffective
Documented Cases: The most famous successful use of this method occurred in the Iliac Bay region during the Second Era, where the destruction of a bloodline founder resulted in the curing of an entire clan. However, more than half of the newly cured vampires died within days due to their advanced age catching up with them instantaneously.
Protective Measures: Awareness of this vulnerability has led many vampire clans to become extremely protective of their eldest members, hiding them in secure locations and surrounding them with layers of protection. Some clans have developed succession rituals to ensure that multiple vampires share the role of blood father, preventing a single death from destroying the entire lineage.
Alchemical and Magical Remedies
Regional Cure Formulations Different regions have developed their own alchemical approaches to treating vampirism, with varying degrees of success:
The Iliac Bay Formula: This recipe has shown effectiveness against several vampire strains native to the region:
* Six cloves of garlic (must be harvested under a full moon)
* Two shoots of bloodgrass (preferably from Morrowind)
* Five leaves of nightshade (picked before dawn)
* Blood of an Argonian (freely given, not taken by force)
* Ashes of a powerful vampire (minimum 100 years undeath)
The preparation requires precise timing and methodology. The ingredients must be combined during specific lunar phases and heated over a flame blessed by a priest of Arkay. Even when prepared correctly, success rates remain below 50% and vary significantly based on the vampire strain being treated.
Cyrodilic Variations: Alchemists in Cyrodiil have experimented with modified versions of the Iliac Bay formula, substituting local ingredients and adding stabilizing agents. These variations show promise but require extensive testing.
Skyrim Adaptations: Nordic alchemists have developed cold-brewing methods that utilize ice and snow in the preparation process, claiming this increases effectiveness against northern vampire strains.
Ritual and Ceremonial Cures
Blood Salt Purification Some traditions involve ritual bathing in specially prepared water mixed with purged blood salts. This process requires:
* Blood salts harvested from vampire remains
* Water from a source blessed by multiple divine traditions
* Ritual purification performed during specific celestial alignments
* Participation of willing clerics from at least three different religious traditions
The ritual must be performed at dawn, symbolizing the vampire's return to the world of daylight and life. Success rates vary wildly, seeming to depend as much on the vampire's willingness to be cured as on the technical aspects of the ritual.
Black Soul Gem Methods Advanced magical practitioners have developed techniques using black soul gems to extract and contain the vampiric essence from infected individuals. This method involves:
* A black soul gem of sufficient size and purity
* Precise magical rituals to separate the vampire's soul from the curse
* Immediate destruction of the gem containing the extracted vampiric essence
* Powerful restoration magic to heal the spiritual damage caused by the separation
This method shows high success rates but requires magical expertise that few possess, and the black soul gems needed are both rare and expensive.
Alternative Curse Displacement
Lycanthropic Replacement In desperate cases, some vampires have sought infection with lycanthropy, hoping that one curse will displace the other. This method is extraordinarily dangerous and unpredictable:
* The interaction between vampirism and lycanthropy is poorly understood
* Some individuals die from the conflict between the two curses
* Others become hybrid creatures with characteristics of both conditions
* A few successfully replace vampirism with lycanthropy, but then face the challenges of their new curse
* In rare cases, the two conditions cancel each other out, resulting in a complete cure
Daedric Curse Substitution Some scholars theorize that other Daedric curses might be used to displace vampirism, but documented attempts are extremely rare and typically end in disaster or death.
Factors Affecting Cure Success
Age of the Vampire: Newly turned vampires are generally easier to cure than ancient ones, as the curse has had less time to integrate with their essence.
Willingness: Vampires who genuinely desire to be cured show higher success rates than those coerced into treatment.
Strain Compatibility: Each vampire strain responds differently to various cure methods, making accurate diagnosis crucial.
Magical Environment: Cures attempted in areas with strong divine presence or magical stability show improved success rates.
Multiple Treatment Approach: Combining several methods simultaneously sometimes increases effectiveness but also raises the risk of fatal complications.
The Mysterious Nature of Cure Reliability
The unreliability of vampiric cures stems from several interconnected factors that make each case unique:
Bloodline Variations: With dozens of known vampire bloodlines and potentially hundreds of minor strains, no single cure can address all possible variations of the curse.
Individual Adaptation: Each vampire's body and soul adapt to the curse differently, creating personalized variations that resist standardized treatments.
Curse Evolution: Vampirism appears to evolve over time, developing resistance to previously effective treatments and requiring constant refinement of cure methodologies.
Incomplete Knowledge: Much vampire lore has been lost or deliberately destroyed over the centuries, leaving modern practitioners working with incomplete information.

Chapter V: The Vampire Aristocracy - Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour
Defining the Elite
Within vampire society, a strict hierarchy exists that elevates certain individuals above their transformed brethren. At the apex of this supernatural aristocracy stand two closely related groups: the Pure Bloods and the Daughters of Coldharbour. These vampires hold elevated status not through age, power, or accumulated wealth, but through their direct connection to the very source of vampirism itself.
Pure Bloods represent a blanket classification for all vampires who received their condition directly from Molag Bal through pact, ritual, or divine intervention. These individuals never experienced mortality in the traditional sense—their vampirism was not a disease contracted from another vampire but a transformation granted (or inflicted) by the Daedric Prince himself.
Daughters of Coldharbour specifically refers to women who were subjected to the same horrific treatment that created Lamae Bal. These individuals were brutalized by Molag Bal directly, dying from their ordeal only to be reborn as vampires. The title "Daughter of Coldharbour" serves as both a mark of the highest vampiric status and a reminder of the terrible price paid for such elevation.
The Creation Process
The process by which one becomes a Pure Blood or Daughter of Coldharbour remains largely shrouded in mystery, as survivors of such encounters are rare and often reluctant to discuss their experiences. However, historical records and testimonies have provided some insight into these transformations.
Pacts with Molag Bal: Some Pure Bloods actively sought out their transformation, making bargains with the Daedric Prince in exchange for power, immortality, or revenge. These individuals typically offered something of great value—their mortal life, their soul, their service for eternity, or the promise to corrupt others in Molag Bal's name. The pacts are binding on a cosmic level, making escape or renegotiation virtually impossible.
Ritual Transformation: Certain cults dedicated to Molag Bal have developed elaborate rituals designed to attract the Daedric Prince's attention and earn his "blessing" of vampirism. These ceremonies often involve:
* Systematic corruption of holy sites or temples
* Mass sacrifice of innocents
* Desecration of symbols sacred to Arkay
* Performance of acts that mock the natural cycle of life and death
* Voluntary submission to Molag Bal's will
Divine Selection: In some cases, Molag Bal appears to choose individuals seemingly at random, transforming them without any apparent request or bargain on their part. These forced transformations often target individuals whose corruption would serve the Daedric Prince's broader goals—respected leaders, powerful mages, or influential religious figures whose fall would create maximum chaos and despair.
The Unique Nature of Their Condition
Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour differ fundamentally from other vampires in several crucial ways:
No Disease Progression: Unlike vampires created through transmission, Pure Bloods experienced no gradual infection period. Their transformation was instantaneous and complete, often accompanied by immediate awareness of their new abilities and nature.
Enhanced Base Abilities: All Pure Bloods possess supernatural capabilities that exceed those of typical vampires from the moment of their creation. These abilities include:
* Greater physical strength and speed
* More potent magical abilities
* Enhanced resistance to traditional vampire weaknesses
* Unique powers specific to their individual relationship with Molag Bal
Bloodline Foundation: Pure Bloods serve as the fountainheads for new vampire bloodlines. Those they subsequently turn often inherit modified versions of their creator's abilities, leading to the development of distinct vampire clans with shared characteristics.
Cosmic Significance: The existence of Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour serves Molag Bal's cosmic agenda of mocking Arkay's domain. Each represents a permanent victory over the natural order, a soul that will never find peace in death.
Powers and Abilities
The supernatural capabilities of Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour vary significantly among individuals, often reflecting the specific nature of their relationship with Molag Bal or the circumstances of their transformation. However, certain common traits appear across most members of this elite group:
Command Over Lesser Vampires: Pure Bloods possess natural authority over vampires they did not personally create. This influence ranges from subtle social dominance to outright psychic control, depending on the Pure Blood's age and power.
Daywalking: Many Pure Bloods can function during daylight hours without the severe penalties that affect other vampires. Some experience mild discomfort, while others show no negative effects whatsoever from sun exposure.
Enhanced Vampiric Drain: Their feeding abilities often extend beyond simple blood consumption to include draining of magical energy, life force, or even memories and emotions from their victims.
Supernatural Charisma: Pure Bloods typically possess an otherworldly magnetism that makes them naturally persuasive and attractive to mortals, facilitating both feeding and the recruitment of servants.
Resistance to Holy Magic: Their direct connection to Molag Bal grants them enhanced resistance to divine magic and blessed items, though they remain vulnerable to particularly powerful holy artifacts.
Longevity Beyond Normal Vampires: While all vampires can achieve great age, Pure Bloods seem to suffer less from the psychological deterioration that sometimes affects ancient vampires, maintaining their sanity and purpose across millennia.
Social Position and Influence
Within vampire society, Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour occupy positions of unquestioned authority and respect:
Natural Leaders: Most vampire clans are led by Pure Bloods or trace their leadership lineage back to them. Their authority is rarely challenged, as other vampires instinctively recognize their superior status.
Arbiters of Vampire Law: When disputes arise between vampire clans or individuals, Pure Bloods often serve as judges and mediators, their decisions carrying the implicit authority of Molag Bal himself.
Guardians of Knowledge: Many of the oldest vampire traditions, rituals, and secrets are maintained and transmitted by Pure Bloods, who serve as living libraries of vampiric lore.
Diplomatic Representatives: When vampire clans need to negotiate with mortal authorities, Daedric cults, or other supernatural entities, Pure Bloods typically serve as ambassadors due to their enhanced charisma and authority.
The Burden of Elite Status
Despite their elevated position, Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour face unique challenges and burdens:
Cosmic Responsibility: They bear direct responsibility for advancing Molag Bal's agenda, often finding themselves compelled to corrupt others or spread vampirism even when they might prefer a more peaceful existence.
Target for Enemies: Their high profile makes them priority targets for vampire hunters, Daedra opposed to Molag Bal, and mortal authorities seeking to strike at vampire leadership.
Isolation from Humanity: Their direct connection to a Daedric Prince makes it nearly impossible for them to maintain meaningful relationships with mortals or find acceptance in mortal society.
Burden of Leadership: Other vampires constantly seek their guidance, protection, or approval, making solitude and personal peace difficult to achieve.
Historical Examples
Throughout Tamriel's history, several notable Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour have shaped the course of events:
Lamae Bal: The first and most famous Daughter of Coldharbour, whose initial resistance and eventual embrace of her condition established many of the patterns followed by later elite vampires.
Lord Harkon: The ancient vampire lord of Skyrim, whose direct pact with Molag Bal granted him the power of a Vampire Lord and enabled his centuries-long quest to blot out the sun.
Serana: Harkon's daughter and a Daughter of Coldharbour who ultimately chose to oppose her father's apocalyptic plans, demonstrating that even elite vampires can resist their cosmic programming.
Count Ravenwatch: A Pure Blood who attempted to lead his clan toward coexistence with mortals, representing the possibility of redemption even among the vampire aristocracy.
The Continuing Legacy
The existence of Pure Bloods and Daughters of Coldharbour ensures that vampirism will never be completely eradicated from Tamriel. As long as Molag Bal continues to create new elite vampires, new bloodlines will emerge, and the curse will adapt and evolve. Their presence serves as a constant reminder that vampirism is not merely a disease to be cured but a cosmic force that actively shapes the world's supernatural landscape.

Chapter VI: The Great Vampire Bloodlines of Tamriel
Lamae Bal's Legacy: The Original Bloodline
The descendants of Lamae Bal represent the oldest and most prestigious vampire bloodline in existence. Their strain, known as Noxiphilic Sanguivoria, carries unique characteristics that set them apart from all other vampires and cement their position as the progenitors of vampiric society.
Unique Abilities of Lamae's Descendants:
* Daywalking: Unlike most vampires, members of this bloodline can walk freely in daylight without suffering any adverse effects from sun exposure
* Enhanced Nocturnal Powers: While unaffected by sunlight, they grow significantly stronger during nighttime hours, with their abilities reaching peak effectiveness under moonlight
* Master Invisibility: They possess unparalleled skill in becoming invisible, able to vanish completely from sight even while moving or taking action
* Blood Supremacy: Their vampiric drain abilities are more potent than other bloodlines, able to sustain themselves on smaller quantities of blood while gaining greater nourishment
The Cult of Scions During the Second Era, Lamae Bal's bloodline experienced a renaissance through the formation of a powerful cult dedicated to her worship. Mortals who sought transformation could undergo the Rite of the Scion, a horrific ceremony where the aspirant's blood was completely replaced with blood drawn directly from Lamae herself.
The ritual process involved:
* Ritual purification through acts that honored Lamae's suffering and transformation
* Complete exsanguination of the candidate
* Transfusion with Lamae's actual blood, often preserved through dark magic
* A period of death and resurrection that mirrored Lamae's original transformation
* Awakening as a "Scion" rather than a typical vampire
Scion Philosophy and Beliefs: Scions viewed themselves as fundamentally superior to other vampires, whom they dismissed as "mere disease victims." Their core beliefs included:
* Vampires created through normal transmission were pitiable creatures suffering from an affliction
* Scions were blessed directly by the Blood Matron with divine vampiric essence
* Their blood was more potent, their wrath more terrible, and their forms more beautiful than common vampires
* They served as Lamae's chosen instruments for spreading "true" vampirism throughout the world
The Doctrine of Dual Hatred: Uniquely among vampire bloodlines, Lamae's descendants were taught to despise both Arkay and Molag Bal equally:
* Hatred of Arkay: For abandoning Lamae in her hour of greatest need and refusing to reverse her transformation
* Hatred of Molag Bal: For the original violation that began the cycle of vampiric existence
* Sacred Feeding: Scions were encouraged to feed preferentially upon followers of both deities as an act of eternal vengeance
This dual hatred made them more radical and unpredictable than other vampire clans, as they rejected both the natural order represented by Arkay and the diabolic authority of their ultimate creator.
The Iliac Bay Bloodlines: A Complex Tapestry
The Iliac Bay region stands as perhaps the most vampire-infested area of Tamriel, home to numerous distinct bloodlines that have coexisted, competed, and warred with each other for centuries. These clans represent some of the oldest vampire societies outside of Lamae Bal's direct lineage, with many tracing their origins to the survivors of the original Nedic massacre.
The Founding Migration: Historical records suggest that members of the Nedic tribes who survived Lamae's initial rampage fled westward to the Iliac Bay region. Some carried wounds from their encounters with the first vampire, and these injuries eventually manifested as vampirism. Over generations, these infected survivors founded distinct clans, each developing unique characteristics based on their individual experiences and the regional influences they encountered.
The Major Iliac Bay Clans
Clan Lyrezi - Masters of Stealth The Lyrezi vampires have developed abilities perfectly suited for covert operations and hidden influence:
* Magical Silence: They can create zones of absolute silence around themselves, making their movements undetectable
* Enhanced Invisibility: Their invisibility powers surpass those of most other clans, allowing them to remain hidden even while interacting with objects
* Shadow Manipulation: Advanced Lyrezi can manipulate shadows and darkness to aid in concealment
* Territory: Primarily concentrated in the darker, more remote regions of High Rock
Clan Selenu - The Fire-Resistant Named after a tribal elder from ancient Skyrim who ordered Lamae's attempted cremation, the Selenu carry both honor and burden in their name:
* Elemental Resistance: They possess strong resistance to fire, frost, and shock damage
* Climate Adaptation: Members can function effectively in extreme weather conditions
* Enhanced Endurance: Their resistance extends to fatigue and exhaustion, allowing for extended activity periods
* Territory: Spread throughout both Hammerfell and High Rock, particularly in mountainous regions
Clan Vraseth - The Swift Hunters The Vraseth have evolved into the most physically agile of the Iliac Bay clans:
* Supernatural Nimbleness: Their speed and agility exceed even typical vampire standards
* Enhanced Reflexes: They can react to threats with lightning-fast responses
* Climbing and Jumping: Capable of scaling walls and leaping great distances
* Territory: Urban areas where their mobility gives them advantages in complex terrain
Clan Montalion - The Mystic Wardens Perhaps the most magically adept of the Iliac Bay clans:
* Teleportation: They can instantly transport themselves across moderate distances
* Paralysis Immunity: They cannot be paralyzed and can cure paralysis in others
* Magical Healing: Enhanced ability to heal both themselves and others
* Territory: Ancient ruins and sites of magical significance
Clan Anthotis - The Scholar Lords The intellectual elite among Iliac Bay vampires:
* Enhanced Intelligence: Members display superior analytical and reasoning abilities
* Memory Palace: Perfect recall of information acquired during their vampiric existence
* Arcane Understanding: Instinctive comprehension of magical principles and theories
* Territory: Libraries, universities, and centers of learning
Clan Garlythi - The Shielded Ones Masters of magical defense and protection:
* Magical Shielding: Can create powerful barriers against physical and magical attacks
* Damage Absorption: Their shields can absorb damage and convert it to beneficial energy
* Ward Mastery: Experts in creating lasting protective enchantments
* Territory: Fortified locations and defensive positions
Clan Haarvenu - The Destructive Force Specialists in combat magic and elemental destruction:
* Elemental Mastery: Exceptional skill with fire, frost, and shock magic
* Destruction Focus: All their magical abilities center around causing damage
* Spell Amplification: Their destructive spells are more powerful than those cast by other vampires
* Territory: Battlefields and sites of historical conflicts
Clan Keerilth - The Immobilizers Masters of controlling and restraining their enemies:
* Paralysis Powers: Can inflict various forms of paralysis on their victims
* Movement Restriction: Abilities extend to slowing, binding, and restraining targets
* Crowd Control: Capable of affecting multiple enemies simultaneously
* Territory: Areas where controlling large groups of people provides advantages
Clan Thrafey - The Healers The most benevolent of the Iliac Bay clans, focused on restoration and healing:
* Tissue Restoration: Exceptional ability to heal wounds and repair damage
* Disease Resistance: They can cure diseases and resist most ailments
* Regeneration: Enhanced healing abilities that exceed normal vampire standards
* Territory: Often found near settlements where they can practice healing
Shared Characteristics and Vulnerabilities
Despite their individual specializations, all Iliac Bay clans share certain fundamental traits:
Common Abilities:
* Enhanced physical capabilities typical of all vampires
* Resistance to most mortal diseases and poisons
* Extended lifespans measured in centuries
* Ability to sense other vampires of their region
The Fatal Flaw: The most significant vulnerability shared by all Iliac Bay clans is their dependence on their bloodline founders. The death of a clan's original vampire will immediately purge vampirism from all descendants, regardless of how many generations removed they are from the founder.
This weakness has had profound effects on Iliac Bay vampire society:
* Extreme Protectiveness: Clans go to extraordinary lengths to protect their eldest members
* Reclusive Behavior: Many clans have become secretive and isolationist to hide their founders
* Succession Planning: Some clans have developed rituals to share the "founder" status among multiple members
* Constant Vigilance: The threat of assassination has made these vampires extremely paranoid and security-conscious
Inter-Clan Relations
The proximity of so many vampire clans in the Iliac Bay has created a complex web of relationships:
Traditional Rivalries:
* Lyrezi and Anthotis compete for influence in scholarly and political circles
* Vraseth and Haarvenu often clash due to their aggressive natures
* Selenu and Montalion maintain an ancient grudge over territorial disputes
Unlikely Alliances:
* Thrafey and Garlythi often cooperate, with healing and protection complementing each other
* Anthotis and Montalion share knowledge and magical research
* Keerilth and Lyrezi work together when stealth and control are both needed
Notable Historical Events
The Montclair Uprising: Baron Montclair's attempt to heal his ailing wife using the corrupted Ayleid relic known as the "Lightless Remnant" represents one of the most significant events in Iliac Bay vampire history. The relic's corruption transformed his entire family into vampires, with his wife becoming a blood fiend—a feral vampire consumed by endless hunger.
Rather than seeking a cure, the Montclair family embraced their transformation, viewing themselves as ascended beings superior to mortals. They launched a campaign to dominate Rivenspire, feeding openly on the local population and establishing a vampire-ruled territory.
Their reign of terror ended when the Vestige, a hero of great power, tracked down and destroyed the entire family. The Montclair incident served as a cautionary tale about the dangers of unchecked vampire ambition and the consequences of embracing the curse too completely.
The Ravenwatch Experiment: Count Ravenwatch, a Pure Blood vampire of considerable age and power, attempted something unprecedented in vampire history: leading his clan toward peaceful coexistence with mortals. His philosophy centered on the belief that vampires could overcome their predatory nature through discipline and alternative feeding methods.
The Ravenwatch clan experimented with:
* Feeding only on criminals and those who consented
* Using animal blood as a primary sustenance source
* Developing meditation techniques to control bloodlust
* Establishing agreements with mortal authorities for mutual benefit
While the experiment showed promise, it ultimately faced opposition from both mortal authorities who refused to trust vampires and other vampire clans who viewed such behavior as weakness or betrayal of their nature.
The Cyrodiil Vampire Order: Masters of Hidden Control
In stark contrast to the numerous competing clans of the Iliac Bay, Cyrodiil hosts only one major vampire organization: the mysterious group known as the Cyrodiil Vampire Order or simply "the Order." This highly organized and disciplined society represents perhaps the most successful example of vampire integration into mortal civilization.
Philosophy and Organization
The Principle of Hidden Authority: The Order operates on the fundamental belief that vampires can achieve the greatest success by remaining completely hidden while exercising subtle control over mortal society. Their core tenets include:
* Never reveal vampiric nature to mortals under any circumstances
* Feed only on sleeping victims who will not remember the encounter
* Accumulate wealth, power, and influence through patient, long-term planning
* Eliminate any vampire who threatens the Order's secrecy
Hierarchical Structure: The Order maintains a complex hierarchical structure based on age, achievement, and adherence to their principles:
* The Inner Circle: Ancient vampires who founded the Order and set its policies
* Senior Members: Vampires with centuries of experience who oversee regional operations
* Active Members: The core body of the Order, responsible for day-to-day operations
* Probationary Members: Newly inducted vampires undergoing training and evaluation
* Associates: Non-vampire mortals who serve the Order knowingly or unknowingly
Admission Requirements: Joining the Order requires more than simply being a vampire. Candidates must demonstrate:
* Superior intelligence and self-control
* Significant personal wealth or useful skills
* Complete commitment to the Order's principles of secrecy
* Willingness to eliminate other vampires who threaten the organization
Methods and Practices
Feeding Protocol: Order members follow strict guidelines for sustenance:
* Feed only during the victim's deepest sleep cycle
* Take only small amounts of blood to avoid detection
* Use subtle magic to prevent memory formation
* Maintain detailed records to avoid feeding from the same person too frequently
* Prefer wealthy or influential victims whose blood provides better nourishment
Wealth Accumulation: The Order has perfected the art of building generational wealth:
* Invest in long-term ventures that mortals cannot see to completion
* Manipulate markets through patient positioning and insider knowledge
* Acquire property and assets during times of chaos when prices are low
* Maintain multiple identities to prevent suspicion about their extended lifespans
Political Influence: Order members systematically infiltrate mortal power structures:
* Place members in positions of administrative authority
* Cultivate relationships with influential mortals
* Fund political campaigns and social movements that serve their interests
* Gather compromising information on key figures for use as leverage
Religious and Philosophical Beliefs
Worship of Molag Bal: The Order maintains traditional reverence for Molag Bal as the "Kin Father," acknowledging their debt to the Daedric Prince while maintaining independence in their operations. Their worship includes:
* Private shrines hidden within their estates and meeting places
* Ritual ceremonies performed during significant astronomical events
* Offerings of particularly choice victims on holy days
* Prayers for continued success in their hidden endeavors
Respect for Clavicus Vile: Unusually among vampire organizations, the Order also shows respect for Clavicus Vile, the Daedric Prince of bargains and wishes. This reverence stems from their appreciation for:
* The art of making profitable deals and agreements
* The importance of careful wording in contracts and negotiations
* The value of granting mortals their desires in exchange for greater gains
* The philosophy that every interaction should benefit the vampire more than the other party
Relations with Other Vampires
Contempt for "Barbaric" Vampires: The Order views most other vampire groups as primitive and counterproductive:
* Openly aggressive clans are seen as threats to vampire survival
* Feral vampires are eliminated whenever encountered
* Nomadic vampires are discouraged from operating in Order territory
* Any vampire activity that attracts attention is ruthlessly suppressed
The Whet-Fang Exception: The Order holds the Whet-Fang vampires of Black Marsh in higher regard than other clans, viewing them as showing "signs of enlightenment." This respect stems from the Whet-Fang's:
* Sophisticated feeding methods using captive victims
* Organized approach to sustenance management
* Ability to operate without attracting mortal attention
* Development of sustainable feeding practices
Elimination Protocols: When other vampires threaten the Order's operations, they employ systematic elimination methods:
* Intelligence gathering to identify all members of the threatening group
* Careful planning to eliminate targets without creating evidence
* Use of mortal agents and hired killers to maintain distance
* Cleanup operations to remove any trace of vampire activity
Territories and Strongholds
Urban Integration: Order members prefer to operate in major cities where they can:
* Blend into diverse populations more easily
* Access wealthy feeding targets
* Maintain multiple safe houses and meeting locations
* Utilize complex financial and legal systems to their advantage
Estate Networks: The Order maintains an extensive network of properties:
* Luxurious urban mansions that serve as primary residences
* Remote country estates for private meetings and ceremonies
* Safe houses strategically located for emergency use
* Commercial properties that generate legitimate income
Hidden Infrastructure: Beneath their public facades, Order properties contain:
* Secret chambers for vampire-specific activities
* Underground tunnels connecting related properties
* Specialized preservation facilities for blood storage
* Communication networks independent of mortal systems
The Black Marsh Whet-Fang: Sustainable Predators
In the hostile swamplands of Black Marsh, a unique vampire clan has developed feeding methods that represent a significant evolution in vampiric survival strategies. The Whet-Fang vampires have earned recognition even from the sophisticated Cyrodiil Order for their innovative approach to sustenance management.
Revolutionary Feeding Methods: Unlike traditional vampires who hunt prey individually and drain victims completely, the Whet-Fang have developed a sustainable system:
Living Blood Banks:
* Capture victims alive rather than killing them during feeding
* Maintain captives under powerful magic-induced comas
* Use the sleeping victims as renewable blood sources
* Establish rotational feeding schedules to prevent victim death from blood loss
* Employ magical stasis to preserve victims for extended periods
Magical Expertise: The Whet-Fang have become masters of specific magical disciplines:
* Stasis Magic: Keeping victims in suspended animation for months or years
* Memory Modification: Ensuring that any conscious moments remain forgotten
* Health Maintenance: Magical techniques to keep captives physically stable
* Illusion Magic: Hiding their operations from potential discoverers
Advantages of Their System:
* Eliminates the risk associated with hunting new victims regularly
* Provides consistent, high-quality blood supply
* Reduces the chance of detection by mortal authorities
* Allows for careful selection of the most suitable victims
* Creates opportunities for long-term planning and stability
Environmental Adaptations: The harsh environment of Black Marsh has shaped the Whet-Fang in unique ways:
* Enhanced resistance to disease and poison from swampland exposure
* Ability to navigate treacherous terrain that deters most intruders
* Skills in swamp survival that allow them to remain hidden for decades
* Adaptation to the region's dangerous wildlife and plant life
Valenwood's Nightmare Clans: Legends Made Real

* Social Integration: Often hold respected positions in forest communities

Verification Challenges: The extreme nature of Valenwood vampire abilities has led some scholars to question whether these reports represent actual vampires or exaggerated folklore. However, several factors support their authenticity:
* Consistent reports across multiple generations

Chapter VII: The Ancient Volkihar Court
The Merethic Era Origins
Among all vampire bloodlines in Tamriel, none can claim greater antiquity or influence than the Volkihar clan of Skyrim. Their leader, Lord Harkon, has walked the earth since the Merethic Era, making him one of the oldest continuously active vampires in recorded history. The clan's longevity and power have allowed them to witness and influence the rise and fall of entire civilizations.
The Founding Pact: Lord Harkon's transformation into a vampire Lord represents one of the most significant individual bargains ever made with Molag Bal. Unlike many vampires who acquired their condition through disease or accident, Harkon actively sought out the Daedric Prince and negotiated directly for power and immortality.
The exact terms of Harkon's pact remain largely unknown, but historical analysis suggests several key elements:
* Personal Transformation: Harkon himself would become a vampire Lord with enhanced abilities
* Family Inclusion: His wife Valerica and daughter Serana would also receive the "blessing" of vampirism
* Eternal Service: The family would serve as agents of Molag Bal's will in Tamriel
* Prophetic Mission: Harkon would be tasked with fulfilling an ancient prophecy to blot out the sun
The Transformation Process: The ritual that transformed the Volkihar family differed significantly from typical vampiric infection:
* All three family members were transformed simultaneously
* The process involved direct interaction with Molag Bal rather than transmission from another vampire
* Each received the full status of Daughter or Lord of Coldharbour
* Their powers exceeded those of typical Pure Blood vampires
Castle Volkihar: Fortress of the Undead
Architectural Marvel: Castle Volkihar stands as perhaps the most impressive vampire stronghold in Tamriel. Built during the early First Era, the castle incorporates both defensive and ceremonial elements:
Physical Structure:
* Gothic Architecture: Towering spires and flying buttresses create an imposing silhouette
* Strategic Location: Positioned on an island in the Sea of Ghosts for maximum isolation
* Defensive Features: Multiple layers of walls, towers, and magical protections
* Underground Complexes: Extensive dungeons and crypts beneath the main structure
Magical Enhancements:
* Ward Systems: Powerful magical barriers protect against intrusion
* Illusion Fields: The castle can be hidden from mortal perception when necessary
* Daedric Influence: Molag Bal's power suffuses the structure, granting additional protections
* Vampire-Specific Features: Rooms and passages designed for undead inhabitants
Interior Arrangements:
* Throne Room: Grand hall where Harkon holds court and conducts clan business
* Library: Extensive collection of vampiric lore, prophecies, and arcane knowledge
* Alchemy Laboratory: Facilities for creating potions and conducting experiments
* Artifact Vaults: Secure storage for powerful vampiric relics and weapons
* Private Quarters: Luxurious accommodations befitting vampire nobility
The Court Hierarchy
Lord Harkon - The Vampire King: As the clan's founder and most powerful member, Harkon rules with absolute authority:
* Ancient Wisdom: Millennia of experience in politics, warfare, and magic
* Vampire Lord Form: Can transform into a powerful winged creature with devastating abilities
* Prophetic Obsession: Single-minded focus on fulfilling the prophecy to eclipse the sun
* Ruthless Leadership: Willing to sacrifice anyone, including family, to achieve his goals
Serana - The Reluctant Princess: Harkon's daughter occupies a unique position within the court:
* Daughter of Coldharbour: Possesses the highest vampiric status
* Ancient Knowledge: Extensive understanding of vampiric history and Elder Scrolls prophecy
* Moral Complexity: Struggles with her vampiric nature and father's extremism
* Powerful Abilities: Master of necromancy and vampiric magic
Valerica - The Exiled Queen: Harkon's wife, though banished, remains a significant figure in clan history:
* Soul Cairn Exile: Voluntarily imprisoned herself to prevent Harkon from completing his prophecy
* Protective Mother: Placed Serana in magical stasis to keep her from Harkon's plans
* Scholarly Pursuits: Extensive research into vampiric curses and potential cures
* Strategic Mind: Centuries of experience in vampire politics and supernatural warfare
The Inner Circle: Several ancient vampires serve as Harkon's closest advisors and enforcers:
* Vingalmo: Master of vampire genealogy and bloodline politics
* Orthjolf: Specialist in combat training and military strategy
* Fura Bloodmouth: Expert in feeding techniques and prey selection
* Garan Marethi: Scholar of ancient lore and magical research
The Tyranny of the Sun Prophecy
The Ancient Prophecy: The central driving force behind Harkon's existence is an ancient prophecy recorded in the Elder Scrolls:
"Among the night's children, a dread lord will rise. In an age of strife, when dragons return to the realm of men, darkness will mingle with light and the night and day will be as one."
Harkon's Interpretation: Lord Harkon believed this prophecy promised that he could permanently eclipse the sun, creating an eternal night that would:
* Allow vampires to operate freely without any daylight limitations
* Establish vampire supremacy over all mortal races
* Create a world-spanning empire ruled by undead nobility
* Fulfill his ultimate service to Molag Bal by corrupting the natural order completely
The Required Components: Fulfilling the prophecy required several specific elements:
* Auriel's Bow: An ancient Elven artifact capable of affecting the sun itself
* Serana's Blood: As a Daughter of Coldharbour, her blood could corrupt blessed weapons
* Specific Timing: The prophecy could only be fulfilled during particular cosmic alignments
* Elder Scroll Knowledge: Understanding the precise methodology required accessing ancient wisdom
The Conflict with Serana: The prophecy's requirements created an irreconcilable conflict between father and daughter:
* Serana's participation was essential but not willing
* The process would likely result in her death or permanent corruption
* Her moral objections to eternal night put her in direct opposition to Harkon's goals
* The family's unity, once their greatest strength, became their greatest weakness
Vampire Lord Abilities
The Volkihar clan's most distinctive feature is their ability to assume Vampire Lord form, a transformation that grants incredible power:
Physical Transformation:
* Winged Form: Bat-like wings enable flight and aerial combat
* Enhanced Size: The vampire grows significantly larger and more imposing
* Clawed Extremities: Razor-sharp claws capable of tearing through armor
* Monstrous Visage: Face becomes more bat-like with enhanced fangs and glowing eyes
* Armored Hide: Skin becomes tough and resistant to conventional weapons
Enhanced Abilities:
* Flight: Complete aerial mobility for combat and travel
* Vampiric Drain: Massively enhanced ability to drain life, magicka, and stamina
* Summon Gargoyles: Can call forth stone guardians to aid in battle
* Corpse Curse: Ability to reanimate multiple corpses simultaneously
* Mist Form: Can become incorporeal to avoid damage and pass through barriers
Magical Powers:
* Vampiric Grip: Telekinetically grasp and lift enemies
* Supernatural Strength: Physical power that can crush stone and bend metal

Chapter VIII: The Akaviri Connection - Tsaesci and Vampiric Artifacts
The Mysterious Tsaesci
Beyond the borders of Tamriel, on the distant continent of Akavir, dwell creatures that share striking similarities with Tamrielic vampires while remaining fundamentally different. The Tsaesci, commonly referred to as "vampiric serpent-folk," represent one of the most intriguing parallels to vampirism found outside Tamriel proper.
Physical Descriptions and Capabilities: Historical accounts of the Tsaesci describe beings that combine serpentine and vampiric characteristics:
* Serpentine Lower Bodies: Many accounts describe them as having snake-like lower halves
* Humanoid Upper Torsos: Their upper bodies appear more human-like in structure
* Enhanced Longevity: They possess lifespans that far exceed normal mortal limits
* Blood Dependency: They require blood for sustenance, though the exact mechanism differs from Tamrielic vampires
* Supernatural Abilities: Various powers including enhanced strength, speed, and mystical capabilities
Cultural and Military Organization: The Tsaesci have demonstrated sophisticated military and political structures:
* Imperial Ambitions: Historical attempts to invade and conquer Tamriel
* Advanced Warfare: Military tactics and weapons that impressed even experienced Tamrielic commanders
* Cultural Complexity: Evidence of sophisticated art, philosophy, and social organization
* Magical Development: Advanced understanding of enchantment and weapon creation
The Connection Question
Scholarly Debate: The relationship between Tsaesci and Tamrielic vampirism remains a subject of intense scholarly debate:
Arguments for Connection:
* Blood Dependency: Both require blood for sustenance and enhanced abilities
* Supernatural Longevity: Extended lifespans beyond normal mortal limits
* Enhanced Physical Capabilities: Strength and speed exceeding normal beings
* Weapon Creation: Both create artifacts that hunger for blood and life force
Arguments Against Connection:
* Different Origins: Tsaesci appear to be a naturally evolved species rather than cursed beings
* Serpentine Nature: Their reptilian characteristics don't match known vampire physiology
* Cultural Differences: Their society lacks the typical vampire clan structures
* Magical Variations: Their supernatural abilities operate through different mechanisms

Generational Planning:

Conclusion: The Eternal Night
As we reach the end of this comprehensive study, it becomes clear that vampirism represents far more than a simple supernatural affliction. It is a fundamental force that has shaped Tamrielic civilization for millennia, influencing everything from individual relationships to the rise and fall of empires.
The Complexity of Undeath
The vampire stands as perhaps the most complex supernatural being in all of Tamriel. Neither fully alive nor completely dead, neither entirely human nor wholly monster, vampires embody the contradictions and complexities that define existence itself. They retain the memories, personalities, and capabilities of their mortal lives while gaining abilities that transcend natural limitations. This duality creates beings capable of both magnificent achievement and terrible atrocity, often within the same individual.
The Individual Vampire: Each vampire represents a unique response to the challenge of undead existence. Some, like Lord Harkon, embrace their supernatural nature completely, pursuing power and dominance with single-minded determination. Others, like Count Ravenwatch, seek to maintain their humanity and find peaceful coexistence with mortals. Still others, like the members of the Cyrodiil Order, develop sophisticated strategies for thriving within mortal society while pursuing their own agenda.
The Collective Impact: Beyond individual stories, vampire society as a whole has created a shadow civilization that parallels and influences mortal development. Vampire clans serve as alternative power structures, vampire economics affect mortal markets, and vampire culture contributes to the arts and sciences. The complex web of relationships between vampire bloodlines, their interactions with mortal governments, and their role in historical events demonstrates that undead society is as complex and nuanced as any mortal civilization.
Lessons for Mortals
Understanding the Threat: For mortal readers of this codex, the most important lesson is that vampires cannot be dismissed as simple monsters or supernatural curiosities. They are intelligent, organized, and possess capabilities that far exceed mortal limitations. Any mortal community or individual that encounters vampires must be prepared to deal with beings who may have centuries of experience, supernatural abilities, and resources accumulated over multiple generations.
Recognizing Complexity: However, mortals must also recognize that vampires are not uniformly evil or necessarily hostile to mortal interests. Many vampires have contributed positively to civilization, some actively protect mortal communities, and others seek redemption or cure for their condition. Blanket hostility toward all vampires may miss opportunities for cooperation and create unnecessary conflicts.
Practical Preparations:
* Education: Understanding vampire abilities, weaknesses, and motivations
* Detection: Learning to identify vampire presence and activities
* Protection: Developing defenses against vampire predation and manipulation
* Cooperation: Recognizing when vampire interests align with mortal goals
* Response: Having appropriate reactions to both hostile and peaceful vampire encounters
Lessons for Vampires
The Importance of Restraint: Vampire history demonstrates repeatedly that excessive ambition and aggression ultimately prove counterproductive. The most successful vampire communities are those that have learned to balance their supernatural advantages with respect for mortal society's values and limitations.
Adaptation and Evolution: The vampire bloodlines and individuals who have thrived over centuries are those who have adapted to changing circumstances while maintaining their core identity. Rigid adherence to ancient traditions or obsessive pursuit of prophectic goals often leads to destruction or irrelevance.
Community and Cooperation: While individual vampires possess formidable abilities, vampire society as a whole is strongest when different bloodlines and communities cooperate rather than compete. The complex challenges of existing in a mortal-dominated world require collective responses and shared resources.
The Eternal Questions
The Nature of Immortality: Vampires represent one answer to the fundamental human question of what it would mean to transcend death. Their experiences suggest that immortality, while offering great opportunities, also creates unique challenges and responsibilities. The vampire condition demonstrates that extending life does not automatically resolve the fundamental questions of meaning, purpose, and moral responsibility.
The Balance of Power: The relationship between vampires and mortals raises profound questions about power, justice, and social organization. How should societies handle citizens who possess supernatural abilities? What rights and responsibilities should apply to beings who can live for millennia? How can democratic institutions function when some participants have vastly different lifespans and capabilities?
The Possibility of Redemption: Perhaps most importantly, vampire existence raises questions about the possibility of redemption and change. If vampires retain their personalities and capacity for growth, can they overcome the predatory nature of their condition? Is it possible for beings created through cosmic violation and supernatural hunger to achieve genuine moral goodness?
Final Reflections
Standing at a crossroads between mortal and divine, between natural and supernatural, between civilization and savagery, vampires embody many of the fundamental tensions that define existence in Tamriel. They serve as dark mirrors reflecting both the best and worst aspects of mortal nature, enhanced by supernatural power and extended across centuries of existence.
For the scholar, they represent an endlessly fascinating subject of study. For the mortal citizen, they represent both threat and opportunity. For the vampire themselves, they represent a condition that must be navigated with wisdom, restraint, and consideration for both their own nature and the broader world in which they exist.
The Continuing Story: As Tamriel continues to evolve and change, so too will vampire society. New technologies, changing social conditions, and evolving relationships between different communities will create new challenges and opportunities for vampires and mortals alike. The story of vampirism in Tamriel is far from over, and each new generation will write its own chapter in this complex and continuing narrative.
A Final Warning: Remember, gentle reader, that knowledge alone is not protection. When you walk the streets of any city at night, when you travel the lonely roads between settlements, when you explore the ancient ruins and forgotten places of Tamriel, remember that somewhere in the shadows, immortal eyes may be watching. Ancient intelligence may be evaluating your worth as prey, servant, or ally. The wise traveler carries both knowledge and blessed silver, for in the realm of the vampire, preparation and understanding are the only shields against eternal damnation.
The night belongs to the vampire, but with wisdom and vigilance, the day can still belong to the living.

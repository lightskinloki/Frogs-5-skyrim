Preface on the Nature of Sound
To the uneducated, the Bard is a minstrel—a fool with a lute who sings for copper. To the educated, the Bard is a historian. But to the Tonal Architect, the Bard is a physicist.
The reality of the Mundus is often mistaken for matter. Stones, trees, flesh—these are perceived as solid objects. This is a sensory illusion. The Dwemer understood the truth: Reality is a Song.
Matter is merely a "Standing Wave"—a note held for so long by the Earth Bones that it has calcified into geology. Gravity, heat, and time are simply the rhythm of the Aurbis. To practice Tonal Architecture is to rewrite the sheet music of local reality. We do not beg the laws of physics to bend; we play a resonant note, forcing the local reality to harmonize at a new frequency.
________________


The Distinction from Magicka
Mages believe that reality is shaped by Magicka—energy flowing from Aetherius. They are partially correct, but they mistake the fuel for the engine.
When a Mage casts a fireball, they are expending internal energy (Magicka) to request a thermal change from the spirits of Aetherius. It drains the soul.
When an Architect plays a Chord, they use no Magicka. They use Breath (Will) and Mathematics. They identify the resonant frequency of the target and project a counter-frequency. It drains the lungs and the mind, but leaves the Magicka well untouched. It is a feat of skill, not sorcery.

The Three Great Movements
Tonal Architecture is divided into three primary theoretical frameworks, or "Movements": Entropy, Harmonics, and Dissonance.
While a Master Architect may eventually command the full spectrum of sound, the initiate must approach them sequentially. To attempt to hold the frequency of destruction and the frequency of stasis in the mind simultaneously without proper conditioning is to invite Tonal Dissonance—a fracturing of the psyche where the mind tries to reject its own existence.
Therefore, the student is instructed to master these Movements one at a time. Mastery is not achieved through reading; it is achieved through Field Composition.
The Methodology of Composition
To project a Tonal manipulation, you must first possess the "Reference Data." You cannot sing a song of shattering iron if you do not understand the sound of iron breaking.
1. Observation: You must witness a moment of extreme physical or emotional resonance in the field (combat).
2. Isolation: You must mentally isolate the specific frequency of that event—the crack of bone, the silence of terror, the thrum of a shield wall.
3. Transcription: You must translate that raw sensory data into musical notation on the provided staves. Only once the song is written can it be projected back into reality as a weapon.
Your Assignment: As you travel, observe the world. When you witness an event that matches one of the theories below, record it. This book contains blank pages for three compositions. Fill them.

Partition Four - The Theory of Entropy (Destruction)
The Dwemer Theorem: Solidity is an illusion maintained by vibration. Every physical object hums with a specific, binding frequency. To destroy an object, one needs only to project the Inverse Frequency. When the external sound meets the internal vibration, the object rejects its own structural integrity.
* Field Data Required: You cannot compose the song of breaking if you do not know the pitch of the break. You must witness a moment of catastrophic kinetic failure. Listen for the specific snap of bone, the grind of steel yielding, or the crack of stone.
* Compositional Method: Isolate that sound of failure. Transpose it into a minor key. Utilize the "Devil's Interval" (the Tritone) to create a sonic paradox. The song should not resolve; it should hang in the air like a jagged fracture.
* The Result: When played, rivets vibrate loose, leather dries and cracks, and magical wards desynchronize. Targets become structurally unsound (All enemies suffer -3 Damage Reduction (DR) for 1 round. enemies within 10 feet are Stunned).
________________


Partition Five - The Theory of Harmonics (Protection)
The Dwemer Theorem: A "Standing Wave" is a phenomenon where two opposing frequencies cancel each other out, creating a zone of absolute stillness (Zero Point Energy). By projecting a perfect, low-frequency drone, an Architect creates a "buffer" of pressurized air that acts as a physical wall, absorbing kinetic energy before it touches the skin.
* Field Data Required: You must observe the rhythm of endurance. Listen to the breathing of a soldier holding the line against a charge. Feel the heat of a fire that fails to burn. Capture the frequency of something that refuses to move.
* Compositional Method: Construct a low, steady drone in a Major key. The tempo must match the resting heartbeat of a giant (approx. 40 beats per minute). It is not a song of action, but of weight. It must vibrate in the chest cavity of your allies, enforcing a physical rhythm of stability.
* The Result: The air becomes viscous around your allies. Impacts are dampened. Balance is reinforced by sonic pressure. They become immovable objects (All allies gain Resistance to Stagger/Knockdown and +5 Temporary HP for 1 round.).

Partition Six - The Theory of Dissonance (Control)
The Dwemer Theorem: Consciousness is not a soul; it is an electrical melody played by the brain. Fear, bravery, and aggression are simply rhythms. By injecting a Psycho-Acoustic Dissonance—a sound that mathematically contradicts the brain's natural rhythm—an Architect can crash the listener's cognitive functions.
* Field Data Required: You must study the silence of terror. Observe the moment a predator roars and the prey freezes. That split-second of paralysis is a "cognitive gap." You must learn to artificially expand that gap.
* Compositional Method: This is not a melody. It is a scream disguised as a note. Layer a high-frequency shriek (felt in the teeth) over a sub-sonic thrum (felt in the gut). The goal is to overstimulate the amygdala so the brain resets.
* The Result: The enemy's fight-or-flight response malfunctions. The nervous system jams. The target forgets how to cast spells, or perform advanced techniques (All enemies must make a Guile Save. On failure, they are Suppressed (Cannot use Magic or FP abilities) for 1 round. on a success they are Terrified).

Instrumentation and Breath
While a lute or flute can create these tones, they lack the volume required for battlefield application. The War Horn is the preferred instrument of the Nord Tongues and the Battle-Bards of old.
Breath Control:
Unlike a spell, which draws from your Magicka reserves, the War Horn draws from your lungs. To sustain a Tonal Manipulation requires immense physical exertion.
* The Technique: You must inhale through the nose for four beats, expanding the diaphragm, and exhale through the horn for eight beats. This creates the continuous pressure needed to bend reality.
* The Cost: Performing these songs requires your full attention. You cannot swing a sword while reshaping the universe. However, because you are using physics and breath, it costs 0 Focus Points (FP).

Partition Eight - The Dwemer Connection
It must be noted that the techniques described in this Primer are crude, biological approximations of the Tonal Resonators used by the Dwemer. The Deep Elves did not shout; they built machines to sing for them. They utilized massive steam-driven pipes and brass tuning forks to warp time and space beneath the earth with mathematical precision. We are forced to use lungs and brass to mimic their equations.
However, the underlying physics remain identical. Whether the note is produced by a steam valve or a throat, if the frequency matches the resonant pitch of the target, the reality of that target must yield. We study the Dwemer to understand the sheet music they left behind.

Note: How This Item Works (Game Mechanics)
What is this?
This book is a Progression Tool. It is how Orion unlocks his Tier 2 abilities for the Bards College Arc.
How does he write a song?
1. The Trigger: Orion must participate in a Major Combat Encounter (A boss fight, a desperate hold-out, or a massive kinetic event like a dragon crash).
2. The Action: During the next "Order of Business" or downtime phase, the player fills out one entry in the "Field Composition Log" based on what happened in that fight.
3. The Reward: Once the entry is filled out, he permanently gains the corresponding ability for his War Horn.
Limitations:
* One Song at a Time: He cannot write all three songs at once. He must experience a new battle to write a new song. (e.g., The fight with Sahloknir allows him to write ONE song now. He must fight another boss to write the second).
* Completion: Once he has written all three songs (Entropy, Harmonics, Dissonance), he has "completed the syllabus" and is ready to advance to Tier 3.
Using the Songs in Combat:
* Cost: 0 FP. (It uses Breath/Physics, not Magicka).
* Action: Major Action. (He trades his attack for the turn to play).
* Sustain: He must continue to use his Major Action each turn to keep playing, or the effect ends.
